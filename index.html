Reason 01 — The Reframe
Probability of Failure → Probability of Adjustment
A 10% chance of a modest, temporary spending adjustment sounds very different from a 10% chance of financial ruin.
Reason 01 — The Reframe

Even a 50% probability of success can be perfectly reasonable — for someone willing to adjust. The median, minimum, and maximum spending over a 30-year journey is remarkably similar whether you targeted 50% or 95%.

What's different? Legacy. One person dies with a much bigger pile of money they never used.

Reason 01 — The Data

Median spending at 20%, 50%, 70%, and 95% probability of success tracks nearly identically across every historical period. The target barely matters.

Reason 01 — The Data

Min and max spending at 50% vs. 95% success — the floors and ceilings are closer than you'd expect. The real cost of targeting 95% is unspent wealth, not security.

Reason 01 — The Data

Portfolio values at 20% vs. 95% success diverge dramatically. Targeting 95% means dying with far more money — wealth you spent your life earning but never lived on.

Reason 02 — The Endowment Effect
Your portfolio is
your territory.

Give half a room a nice notebook. Give the other half nothing. Ask both what it's worth. The owners demand roughly twice the price. Every time.

The real asymmetry of risk
The fear everyone has
Spending too much.
Running out.
The risk nobody fears
Spending too little.
Dying with a fortune unused.
Reason 02 — The Endowment Effect
The asymmetry of your fear
is not rational.
It's evolutionary.

You are not broken for feeling this way. But the mismatch is costing you years of your life.

Reason 03 — The Solution
Risk-Based Guardrails
Your portfolio · Current value
▼ LOWER GUARDRAIL
▲ UPPER GUARDRAIL
$1.2M → Cut 5.3%
You are here
$2.4M → Take a raise
01
Set initial spending using historical analysis
Same technique William Bengen used for the 4% rule.
02
Define your lower guardrail — in specific dollar amounts
When portfolio reaches $X, spending drops by exactly $Y/month.
03
Define your upper guardrail — the raise trigger
When portfolio reaches $Z, time for a raise. You decided in advance.
Reason 03 — In Practice
Market drops.
Two different responses.
Betty — No guardrails
Portfolio goes from $1.8M → $1.6M.
She watches probability of success tick down: 88… 84… 81.
She has no idea what to do. No pre-defined response.
Three months in: browsing job listings.
⚠ Decision made under maximum stress
Wilma — Guardrails defined
Portfolio dips to $1.6M — not even close to her lower guardrail of $1.2M.
She knows exactly what triggers a cut and how much.
She already made that decision in advance. It's in a document.
✓ She goes for a walk. She reads a book. She lives her life.

She doesn't need to make a decision under stress. She already made it.

Reason 03 — Historical Evidence
Backtested against
every crisis on record.
Great Depression Post-War Inflation Stagflation Dot-Com Bubble Global Financial Crisis COVID 2022 Drawdown
The spending adjustments were shockingly small.

Because we can step through these scenarios month by month, we build confidence in advance. The point is not to plan less carefully. The point is to plan differently.
